The Garden Guidebook

A collaborative guidebook for the Garden bouldering area generated using the LocalBoulders framework. The book is a living document which is continuously being updated, downlaod the latest version as a PDF.

About the Project

Scope

The Garden Guidebook is intended to be a comprehensive guide to the climbing at the Garden climbing area outside of Sweet Home, Oregon. Additional sections are included to document other nearby areas, but these areas are not covered in as great of detail. The guidebook is a living document that has been continuously developed since July of 2022. A PDF version is available for free online and a print version is in development.

Information Sources

Much of the information in this book has been sourced from my own first hand knowledge of the area. I have been climbing at the Garden for over a decade and have personally climbed or attempted roughly 75% of the rotues documented in this book. Several other diy guides to the area have been authored over the years as well. The following of these historical guidebooks have been referenced for this project:

  • The Garden Wall (Jayson Nissen, pdf, 2021)
  • Garden Area Bouldering (Cooper Doe, pdf, 2019)
  • The Garden (Paul Waters, unpublished/scanned pdf, circa 2011)
  • The Garden (Eric Brown, unpubished, circa 2001)
  • The Clear Cut (Apocalypse Now) (Eric Brown, unpubished, circa 2001)
  • Gordon Ridge/The Vines (Eric Brown, unpubished, circa 2001)

I have primarily used these texts as references for names and locations, the descriptions and topos in The Garden Guidebook are all original work based on my own observations. I also personally know and have collaborated with some of the authors of these books as well as other developers of the area.

Ways to Contribute

Send Your Feedback

Is there any information in the book that you find confusing? Are there any route names or grades that you disagree with? Is your buddy's sick FA not credited in the index? If you have any thoughts on how the information in the guidebook can be expanded or improved please get in touch with me at an.child@gmail.com.

Call for Photos

A print version of the guidebook is currently in deveolpment. This version will be similar to the regular pdf except it will feature additional action and scenery photos. If you have photos that you want to be considered for the guidebook get in touch at an.child@gmail.com. Anyone whose photo gets included in the guidebook will get a credit in the index and a free copy of the print guide. If you send a photo with a person in it please get their premission before sharing.

Submit Change Request Through Github

If you are familiar with Git you are wellcome to collaborate and submit propossed changes directly on the platform.
